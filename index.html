What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

A helmet is considered old and needs replacing if it's more than 5 years old, shows visible signs of wear and tear like cracks or dents, has a damaged interior or exterior, has worn-out straps, no longer fits properly, or has been involved in a significant crash

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, perform a thorough inspection to ensure it's safe; check for rust, flat tires, worn brakes, loose components, and a properly inflated battery, as long-term storage can significantly impact the bike's condition and make it unsafe to ride without proper maintenance

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, you should always wear a helmet, ride with traffic, follow all traffic signals and stop signs, use hand signals when turning, stay alert, be predictable by riding in a straight line, maintain a safe distance from other vehicles, and use lights and reflective gear when visibility is low; essentially, treat your bike like any other vehicle on the road and follow the rules of the road.
